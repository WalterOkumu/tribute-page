Prof. Wangari Muta Maathai

'In a few decades, the relationship between the environment, resources and conflict may seem almost as obvious as the connection we see today between human rights, democracy and peace.'

- Prof. Wangari Muta Maathai

Here's a timeline of Prof. Wangari Muta Maathai's lift:

  • 1940 - Born in Ihithe village, Tetu division, Nyeri County, Kenya (then known as Nyeri, Kenya Colony)
  • 1943 - Family relocates to white-owned farm in the Rift Valley, near Nakuru town
  • 1947 - Returned to Ihithe with her mother and two brothers to attend Ihithe Primary School
  • 1951 - Moved o St. Cecilia's Intermediate Primary School & joined the Legion of Mary
  • 1956 - Completed her studies, was rated first in her class, and was granted admission to the only Catholic high schoool for girls in Kenya, Loreto High School, Limuru.
  • 1960 - Became one of some 300 Kenyans selected to study in the United States under the The Kennedy Airlift
  • 1960 - Received a scholarship to study at Mount St. Scholastica College (now Benedictine College), in Atchison, Kansas, where she majored in biology, with minors in chemistry and German.
  • 1964 - Received her Bachelor's of Science Degree
  • 1966 - Received her Master's of Science in Biological Sciences and was appointed to a position as a research assistant to the professor of zoology at University College of Nairobi (now University of Nairobi)
  • 1967 - Travelled to Germany to pursue her doctorate and studied both at University of Giessen and Ludwig Maximilian University of Munich
  • 1971 - Became the first Eastern African woman to receive a PhD, with her doctorate in Veterinary Anatomy
  • 1973 - Was a member of the Kenya Red Cross Society, and became it's Director
  • 1974 - Following the establishment of the Environment Liaison Centre, She was asked to be a member of the local board, eventually becoming board chair. The Environment Liaison Centre worked to promote the participation of non-governmental organisations in the work of the United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP)
  • 1976 - Became the first female chair of the Department of Veterinary Anatomy
  • 1977 - Became the first female Associate Professor at University College of Nairobi (now University of Nairobi)
  • 1989 - She learned of a plan by the Government of Kenya to build a 60-storey Kenya Times Media Trust Complex in Uhuru Park
  • 1990 - Successfuly got foreign led investors to cancel the Project due to media pressure and writting multiple letters to UN Chapters
  • 1991 - She received the Goldman Environmental Prize in San Francisco and The Hunger Project's Africa Prize for Leadership in London
  • 1992 - Discovered a plot by the Governement of Kenya to assassinate her and other pro-democracy activists. She and others were later arrested and charged with spreading malicious rumours, sedition and treason. Due to pressure from international organisations and eight senators, including Al Gore and Edward M. Kennedy, the Government of Kenya was forced to substantiate the charges or risk damaging relations with the US. The Governement later dropped the charges.
  • 1993 - While release on bail in 1992, she and others took part in a hunger strick in a corner of Uhuru Park labelled Freedom Corner, to pressure the Governement to release political prisoners. After multiple protests held at the All Saints Cathedral, the prisoners were finally released. She also received the Edinburg Medal and the Jane Addams International Women's Leadership Award.
  • 1998 - She learned of a Government plan to privatize large areas of public land in the Karura Forest, just outside Nairobi. Through letters to the Government and the press, she protested the destruction fo the forest.
  • 2002 - Was elected with an overwhelming 98% of votes cast, as Member of Parliament for Tetu Constituency
  • 2003 - Was appointed Assistant Minister in the Ministry for Environment and Natural Resources
  • 2004 - Was awarded the 2004 Nobel Peace Prize for her contribution to sustainable development, democracy and peace. She is the first African woman to win the award.
  • 2009 - Published The Challenge for Africa, describing her insights into the strengths and weaknesses of governance in Africa, her own experiences, and the centrality of environmental protection to Africa's future.
  • 2011 - Died while receiving treatment at a Nairobi hospital.
